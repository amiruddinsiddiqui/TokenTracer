# ECONOMICS.md

## What a Converted Lead Is Worth

The core assumption is that Credex earns money when startups purchase discounted AI credits or move recurring AI spend through its platform.

Assume:
- average startup team size: 5–15 people
- average monthly AI spend: $300–$1500
- Credex earns roughly 8–15% margin on AI credit transactions or negotiated pricing

If an average customer spends:
- $1000/month on AI tooling
- Credex captures 10% margin

Then:
- monthly revenue per customer ≈ $100
- annual revenue per customer ≈ $1200

Not every audit user becomes a paying customer, so the important number is expected lead value.

Example:
- 100 completed audits
- 15 consultation bookings
- 3 paying customers

If each customer is worth ~$1200/year:
- total value from 100 audits = ~$3600/year
- expected value per completed audit ≈ $36

## Estimated CAC by Channel

### Founder DMs on X
- cost: $0
- time-heavy but highly targeted
- estimated CAC: $20–$60 in founder time

### Reddit / Indie Hackers Posts
- cost: $0
- lower intent but scalable
- estimated CAC: $10–$40

### Shared Audit Links
- strongest organic loop
- users share savings screenshots or reports
- estimated CAC: near $0 if sharing behavior works

### Founder Communities / Discord
- mostly trust-based distribution
- estimated CAC: $15–$50

At this stage, time is the main acquisition cost rather than paid advertising.

## Conversion Math

Assume:
- 100 completed audits
- 20% book consultations
- 20% of consultations become paying customers

That becomes:
- 100 audits
  → 20 consultations
  → 4 paying customers

If each customer generates ~$1200 ARR:
- 4 customers = ~$4800 ARR

That means each completed audit is worth roughly:
- $48 expected ARR

If acquisition cost per audit stays under ~$10–15, the funnel becomes profitable fairly quickly.

## What Would Need To Be True To Reach $1M ARR

Assume average customer value:
- $1200 ARR

To reach $1M ARR:
- roughly 830 active paying customers needed

Possible funnel:
- 100,000 total visitors
- 10,000 completed audits
- 2,000 consultations booked
- 850 paying customers

That requires:
- strong organic sharing
- repeat founder usage
- expansion into team-wide AI spend management
- broader support for APIs and enterprise tooling
- consistent founder community distribution

The biggest requirement is not traffic alone. The tool would need strong audit-sharing behavior and recurring usage around AI spend management as teams adopt more AI subscriptions over time.

---

## Important Assumption

This model only works if AI subscription fragmentation keeps increasing.

If startups continue paying separately for:
- ChatGPT
- Claude
- Cursor
- Gemini
- Copilot
- API credits

then AI spend auditing becomes a real operational problem instead of a temporary trend.

That growing complexity is the main reason the economics could work long term.
