## North Star Metric

The main North Star metric for Token Tracer is:

**Completed audits that generate a public share link**

This matters more than raw traffic or DAU because the product is not something users open daily. It is a high-intent B2B utility tool. A completed audit means the user:
- entered real AI stack information
- received recommendations
- saw potential savings
- reached the point where the report could realistically be shared internally with teammates or founders

Public share generation is important because it combines both product usefulness and organic distribution potential.

---

## Input Metrics

### 1. Audit Completion Rate
Percentage of users who start the audit form and successfully complete it.

This measures:
- onboarding friction
- form UX quality
- perceived product value

Target: >40%

---

### 2. Share Link Generation Rate
Percentage of completed audits that generate or open a public share link.

This measures:
- whether users found the report valuable enough to share
- viral distribution potential

Target: >25%

---

### 3. Consultation Conversion Rate
Percentage of completed audits that lead to consultation interest or email capture.

This measures:
- actual business intent
- monetization potential

Target: >10%

---

## What I’d Instrument First

The first events I would track:
- audit started
- audit completed
- share link opened
- lead form submitted
- consultation CTA clicked
- public share page visited

These events capture the entire lead-generation funnel.

---

## Pivot Decision Trigger

I would seriously reconsider the product direction if:
- fewer than 10% of users completed audits
- almost nobody opened or shared public reports
- consultation conversion stayed below 2–3% after multiple iterations

That would likely mean the pain point is not strong enough or the savings recommendations are not compelling enough for startup teams.
